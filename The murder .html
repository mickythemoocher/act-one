THE MURDER THAT NEVER WAS

Characters

Muriel

Jane

Michael

Alex

The Police Inspector

Five people are gathered together in an old, creaky, country house.

Muriel: Would anyone like a drink?

Michael: Yes please I'll have a scotch

Muriel: Anyone else

Jane: If there is any white wine that would be nice.

Muriel: I'll check. How about you Inspector?

Inspector: (curtly) Not for me thank you, not while I am on duty.

Muriel: Yes of course.

Alex: I would like get this sorted out, rather than start drinking.

Jane: Sort what out?

Alex: Oh for goodness sake. The murder of course

Jane: But Alex, there hasn't been a murder.

Alex: How do we know?

Michael: (Sarcastically)Well let me do a head count. Hmm Muriel, Jane, Alex and myself. Now let me see, that's one two, three, four. We all seem to be here. Please put your hand up if any of you have been murdered. (short pause) I see, no hands up so looks like there was no murder.

Alex: Ha ha ha, very funny. I am trying to be serious.

Muriel: Here is your scotch Michael.

Michael: And so am I , none of us, as yet, have been murdered. Thank you Muriel.

Alex: Suppose it is not one of us?

Muriel: What do you mean Alex?

Alex: Maybe there is someone else in the house, or was.

Muriel: I suppose that is possible, it's a big house.

Jane: Ok, so let's search the house, room by room.

Inspector: I am afraid that is not possible.

Jane: Why not?

Inspector: This is a police matter madam, we cannot allow you to just wander through the rest of the house and possibly contaminate evidence.

Muriel: Here is your wine Jane, sorry I over filled it a little.

Jane: Thanks Moo.

Alex: Well if it is police matter inspector, why are you not policing? If we can't do the search, are you going to?

Inspector: All in good time sir, all in good time.

Alex: And are there any more policemen on the way?

Inspector: Well, we will have to see about that.

Jane: I think the whole thing is rather fun.

Inspector: Rather fun? I am afraid miss that I do not share your opinion that murder is rather fun.

Jane: Oh come on inspector. There has been no murder here. Not even a decent argument.

Muriel: That's true, we all seem to get on rather well.

Michael: I agree with you Jane, this whole thing does seem rather trivial. And before you say it inspector, I don't mean that murder is trivial, just this situation.

Inspector: This situation sir?

Michael: Yes inspector. You seem to be investigating a murder that never was.

Muriel: That is rather cutting Mike. The inspector is just doing his job.

Jane: And it is not a job I would like to do â€“ too grisly

Michael: As far as I can see he is doing no job at all.

Muriel: Well at least let him try to do his job.

Inspector: Quite right miss.

Alex: Well that job doesn't seem to include very much investigating.

Inspector: On the contrary. I may be doing far more than you realise.

Muriel: So what do you have so far? Apart from our names?

Inspector: I have suspicions.

Michael: So inspector, if you are saying you have your suspicions. Does that infer that (a short pause) one of us is your suspect?

Inspector: It infers nothing of the kind sir. I have as yet, no suspects at all.

Alex: Pardon me for butting in inspector but it seems apart from no suspects you also seem to have no body.

Michael: Yes one of the prime ingredients of a murder really I would think.

Inspector: As I said before sir, this is no joke.

Michael: I didn't intend it as a joke and I am sorry if it appeared that way.

Alex: So what is the next move? Are we to be interrogated?

Inspector: You seem in rather a hurry sir. Is there a reason for that?

Alex: No of course not. I just feel that there is very little actually being done.

Inspector: But you feel I should interrogate you all?

Alex: No I don't feel that at all, it was just a question, that is all.

Jane: You can interrogate me if you wish inspector, I have nothing to hide. And I never have.

Muriel: Nor do I. You can do me next. In a way it is quite exciting. I have never been interrogated before. I must be very boring, I never do anything bad.

Inspector: There will be no interrogation, at least not for the time being. First of course there will be the search.

Michael: For a body? I mean that would be a start wouldn't it.

Inspector: I must tell you sir there is often no body to be found in a murder investigation.

Michael: Ok, whatever you say.

Alex: Well I say if you are here to investigate maybe you should start investigating.

Inspector: Once again sir, it seems you are in somewhat of a rush. Do you have something to hide? Something you re not telling us?

Alex. Oh! For goodness sake. Of course not. Why should I have anything to hide just because I want to get this over with?

Inspector: Slowly, slowly catchee monkee. As my grandmother used to say.

Michael: Maybe we need your grandmother here as well then.

Jane: Maybe Inspector, we could all help with the search. I mean under your supervision.

Inspector: Very kind of you to offer Miss but that will not be necessary. Members of the public are rarely helpful in these situations. That has been my past experience anyway.

Muriel: That is a rather scathing comment inspector.

Inspector: I apologise if that sounded rude madam. I just meant that policeman are trained to search properly whereas members of the public are not. It is just a fact of life.

Michael: Trained by your grandmother I assume.

Jane: Don't be rude Michael.

Michael: Well, this whole thing is becoming a bit of a farce.

Inspector: I assure you it isn't sir.

Jane: So what do we do now then? Just sit here and wait to see what happens next?

Inspector: I assure you all I will clear this matter up as quickly as I can.

Michael: Not like the monkey then?

Jane: Michael! Stop it!

Muriel: So if we just have to sit and wait I am going to have another drink. Anyone else?

Jane: Yes please Moo, that wine was rather nice.

Muriel: That's odd! I mean very odd.

Jane, Michael and Alex: (together)What is?

Muriel: Well, you just said that the wine I poured you was rather nice.

Jane: Yes, I did and it was. I was surprised at how nice it was.

Muriel: How do you know? How do you know what it tasted like?

Jane: What do you mean Moo?

Muriel:It is still on the table, you haven't touched it so how could you say it was nice?

(A short pause)

Jane: Errr... I thought I had drunk it. In fact, I am sure I did

Alex: You couldn't have drunk it if it's still on the table.

Jane: I am sure I did.

Michael: look the glass is still completely full.

Inspector: (sharply) Don't touch that glass sir.

Michael. Why not?

Inspector: Evidence sir, and we must not tamper with it.

Alex: (laughing) What evidence. There is no clue there. Jane just forgot that she hadn't drunk her wine.

Jane: I didn't forget. I am so sure I drank it, I remember the taste.

Michael: Forgetting to drink ones wine is hardly a crime inspector. Not one that I have ever hear of anyway.

Jane: I know, I did drink it. I can even remember the taste.

Muriel: Never mind Jane, not a problem. I am going to pour myself one though.

Michael: I'll have a scotch if there is one.

Muriel: There is scotch. In fact there is about every possible drink you can imagine.

Jane: (Quietly) I am sure I did drink it.

Inspector: Please make sure nobody touches that glass.

Alex: It seems inspector that you are clutching at straws if that glass is your only evidence.

Michael: Maybe it is a suspect too. Sorry, I am making jokes again and I shouldn't.

Inspector: You seem determined to make a joke of this sir.

Michael: I am sorry. I am just a little frustrated by all this. Nothing seems to be getting done.

Inspector: You are wrong sir. I feel I am making great progress. I am sure, more than you possibly realise

Jane: Well that's good to hear. I would like to get this over and get out of here. I am finding this whole situation too scary.

Muriel: Yes so am I Jane.

Alex: So will this murderous mystery without a murder soon be solved?

Inspector: As I said sir, I will complete my investigation as quickly as possible.

Jane: I am pleased to hear that.

Inspector: And of course solving one mystery may help solve another.

Alex: What do you mean by that? What other mystery?

Michael: Are the mysteries accumulating then?

Inspector: It would appear so sir.

Alex: Like what?

Inspector: Like the glass of wine sir.

Michael: Ha! Oh no not the glass of wine again .

Inspector: Once again. Not a laughing matter. It is very relevant.

Muriel: In what way?

Jane: Yes, in what way. I want to know because I am convinced I drank it.

Inspector: I believe you

Jane: Do you:

Inspector: I believe you are convinced you drank it. On the other hand, I don't believe you did.

Alex: It seems inspector you are making this more of a mystery than it actually is.

Michael: I'd agree with that.

Inspector: You are both entitled to your opinions of course but for me this investigation is proceeding in a very positive way.

Jane: Because of the wine?

Inspector: Yes, amongst other things.

Alex: Are you able to share with us what those other things are?

Inspector: Not at the moment, I would rather wait until my suspicions are a little more confirmed.

Alex: We wait with bated breath:

Inspector: So my next part of the investigation is the search for the murder weapon. I may not find one but it will be my intention to look in every conceivable nook and cranny.

Michael: Really? You mean you are actually going to search the place?

Inspector:I am indeed sir. And search it very thoroughly indeed.

Muriel: And do you hope to find a body?

Inspector: No, I don't actually. It is the last thing I expect to find.

Alex: Why not? This is a murder investigation isn't it?

Inspector: It is indeed sir, but there has been no murder (Short Pause) Not as yet!

END OF ACT ONE
